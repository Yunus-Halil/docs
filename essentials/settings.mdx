Every Fixiol project has comprehensive statistics that allow you to see the bigger picture and gain an advantage. You can see data fast.

# Weather

Navigate to the weather sidebar in your project. Keep in mind you need to have an address attached to use this feature. Click on the blue "Analyze Weather Data" button.

You should now see a map of all the hail zones. Here's how to understand.

### Green Dots

Green dots represent hail under 1.5 inches

### 

### Yellow Dots

Yellow dots represent hail between 1.5-2 inches in size.

### Red Dots

Red Dots represent hail over 2 inches.

## Filtering Weather Data

![Screenshot2025 08 06at11 03 30AM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-08-06at11.03.30AM.png)

To change the historical times of weather, simply adjust the **Time Range Filter.**

If you want to increase Range, adjust the **Search Range Control slider.**

## Adding Hail Events to your report

Simply click on a hail dot in the map.

<img
  src="/images/Screenshot2025-08-06at11.08.45AM.png"
  alt="Screenshot2025 08 06at11 08 45AM Pn"
  style={{ width:"100%" }}
/>

You can then click the \*\*Include in damage analysis checkbox. \*\*

<Tip>
  The Top 5 Recommended Hail events show the closest hail, allowing you to find relevant hail events faster.
</Tip>

## Property Likelihood of Hail Damage

If you scroll down, you'll see a section that displays a percentage. This percentage shows the likelihood of hail-caused damages. You'll also see a Monthly Weather Events timeline showing the hail events happening each month.

# More Statistics

<ResponseField name="Overview Tab">
  Displays the severity of roof damages, the total damage zones, the percentage of roof damaged, as well as the repair cost.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="Insurance Tab">
  Displays the insurance score, calculated from annotation damage type and severity. Helpful for client roof claims.

  <Danger>
    The insurance score is an estimate. DO NOT use it for claims. We DO NOT guarantee the accuracy of the score.
  </Danger>
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="Images Tab">
  Displays each image inside the folder and allows users to delete images. Also displays drone location of where the images were taken. Simply hover over a marker and the associated image will appear.
</ResponseField>
